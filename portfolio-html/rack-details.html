3D Printed Sample Rack

  • Organization: Sun Research Group
  • Dates: Apr 2022 - Jun 2022
  • Summary: A hot-swappable sample rack I made and 3D printed to help my lab members organize and store their samples.

Overview

The 3D printed sample rack is a simple tool designed to hold sample vials and miniature tubes. It measures around x by y by z centimeters and is assembled by joining two separate components - a lid and a base. The reasoning behind the two-part design was that it would allow the user to swap out the lid to hold different size samples, as well as improve printability by eliminating overhangs. The rack was designed to be used by the PhD students in the Sun lab, who wanted a portable rack that could hold their printed samples. This project was pretty fun due to its consumer-facing nature, and I had to make several design choices to optimize the user experience.

I made two distinct versions of the rack. The first version uses a lego-style connector to join the two components, and relies on an interference fit to stay together. The second version relies on embedded magnets to hold the rack together, which made it easier to swap the lid. The magnets were embedded in all four corners of both components using a press fit. I printed more of the lego-style test racks since they could be assembled immediately after printing, while the magnetic racks required press fitting eight magnets. Furthermore, the lid ended up rarely being swapped, so there was little reason to manufacture more magnetic racks.
